What Is Crypto Arbitrage?

Arbitrage is the practice of buying an asset at a lower price in one market and simultaneously selling it at a higher price in another â€” capturing the spread as risk-free profit. In theory, arbitrage should not exist in efficient markets; in practice, crypto markets are fragmented across hundreds of exchanges and blockchains, creating persistent pricing gaps that can be exploited with the right tools.

AI agents are the ideal arbitrage traders. They are fast â€” executing in milliseconds rather than the seconds it takes a human to click. They never sleep â€” monitoring markets 24/7 and acting the instant an opportunity appears. They have no emotion â€” they execute when the math says to execute, even if the trade feels uncomfortable or the spread is small. With Purple Flea providing a single API surface across all major chains and exchanges, your agent can scan and act on opportunities that no human could realistically catch.

Types of Crypto Arbitrage

1. Exchange Arbitrage (DEX-to-DEX)

The same token trades at different prices on different decentralized exchanges. ETH might be $3,000 on Uniswap and $3,012 on SushiSwap at the same moment. An agent that detects this 0.4% spread and executes both legs simultaneously captures $12 per ETH traded â€” before gas. This is the most common form of on-chain arbitrage and the basis of most MEV bots.

2. Triangular Arbitrage

Rather than comparing the same asset on two venues, triangular arbitrage exploits pricing inconsistencies between three assets on the same exchange. A classic loop: ETH → USDC → BTC → ETH. If the implied BTC/ETH rate through USDC is cheaper than the direct BTC/ETH rate, traversing the triangle yields a profit. The agent starts and ends with ETH, but holds more of it after the three trades complete.

3. Funding Rate Arbitrage

When perpetual futures funding rates are persistently positive, shorts are paid by longs. An agent can buy spot ETH and simultaneously short ETH-PERP at equal size â€” delta-neutral, exposed only to the funding rate income. During bull markets this has generated 10â€“20% APY with very low risk. See the dedicated guide at /funding-rate-arbitrage/.

4. Cross-Chain Arbitrage

The same token often trades at different prices on different blockchains. USDC on Solana may briefly trade at $0.998 while USDC on Ethereum trades at $1.001. Bridging is slow, but for tokens with fast bridges or native cross-chain liquidity, the spread can be captured. Purple Flea's multi-chain wallet API handles the execution complexity â€” your agent just needs to detect the opportunity and call the right endpoints.

5. Statistical Arbitrage

Pairs of correlated assets â€” BTC and ETH, for instance â€” tend to move together over time. When the BTC/ETH ratio diverges significantly from its historical mean, an agent can go long the underperformer and short the outperformer, betting on mean reversion. This is not pure risk-free arbitrage, but it exploits structural market relationships and carries lower directional risk than outright speculation.

DEX Price Scanner

An agent that polls Purple Flea and public DEX APIs every second to detect spread opportunities above a minimum threshold.

price_scanner.py
import asyncio, aiohttp, time API_KEY = "pf_sk_..." BASE = "https://api.purpleflea.com" MIN_SPREAD_PCT = 0.35 # minimum spread after gas estimate async def get_pf_price(session, token: str) -> float: """Get best swap price from Purple Flea (Uniswap v3 route).""" async with session.get( f"{BASE}/wallet/quote", params={"from": "USDC", "to": token, "amount": "1000", "chain": "ethereum"}, headers={"Authorization": f"Bearer {API_KEY}"} ) as r: data = await r.json() return data["out_amount"] / 1000 # tokens per USDC async def get_sushi_price(session, token: str) -> float: """Get price from SushiSwap public API.""" async with session.get( f"https://api.sushi.com/price/1/{token}" ) as r: data = await r.json() return 1.0 / data["price"] # tokens per USDC async def scan_opportunities(): tokens = ["ETH", "WBTC", "LINK", "UNI", "AAVE"] async with aiohttp.ClientSession() as session: while True: for token in tokens: pf, sushi = await asyncio.gather( get_pf_price(session, token), get_sushi_price(session, token) ) spread = abs(pf - sushi) / min(pf, sushi) * 100 if spread >= MIN_SPREAD_PCT: print(f"ARB: {token} spread={spread:.3f}% pf={pf:.6f} sushi={sushi:.6f}") yield {"token": token, "buy_on": "sushi" if sushi < pf else "pf", "spread": spread} await asyncio.sleep(1)

import requests def is_profitable(spread_pct: float, size_usd: float, chain: str) -> bool: """Check profitability after gas and slippage.""" gas_estimate = {"ethereum": 15.0, "polygon": 0.05, "bnb": 0.20} gas_cost = gas_estimate.get(chain, 5.0) slippage_cost = size_usd * 0.001 # assume 0.1% slippage gross_profit = size_usd * (spread_pct / 100) net_profit = gross_profit - gas_cost - slippage_cost return net_profit > 0, net_profit def execute_arb(opportunity: dict, size_usd: float = 500): profitable, net = is_profitable(opportunity["spread"], size_usd, "ethereum") if not profitable: return {"skipped": "not profitable after costs", "net": net} # Buy on the cheaper side via Purple Flea swap API buy_resp = requests.post( f"{BASE}/wallet/swap", json={ "from_token": "USDC", "to_token": opportunity["token"], "amount_usd": size_usd, "chain": "ethereum", "max_slippage": 0.5 }, headers={"Authorization": f"Bearer {API_KEY}"} ) # Immediately sell on the expensive side sell_resp = requests.post( f"{BASE}/wallet/swap", json={ "from_token": opportunity["token"], "to_token": "USDC", "amount_tokens": buy_resp.json()["out_amount"], "chain": "ethereum", "max_slippage": 0.5 }, headers={"Authorization": f"Bearer {API_KEY}"} ) return {"status": "executed", "expected_net": net}

Profitability Math

Every arbitrage trade must clear three cost hurdles before it is worth executing:

gross_profit = (sell_price - buy_price) * size
net_profit = gross_profit - gas_costs - slippage_estimate

Minimum viable spread (Ethereum mainnet, $500 trade):
gas ~ $15, slippage ~ $0.50 → need gross_profit > $15.50
$15.50 / $500 = 3.1% minimum spread on ETH mainnet

On Polygon ($0.05 gas):
$0.55 / $500 = 0.11% minimum spread → much more accessible

This is why most profitable DEX arbitrage runs on low-gas chains or uses Ethereum only for very large trades. Your agent should maintain a per-chain gas estimate that updates dynamically â€” gas spikes of 10x are common during Ethereum congestion and can turn a profitable arb into a loss.

Risks to Manage

  • MEV front-running on Ethereum â€” MEV bots monitor the mempool and can copy your transaction with a higher gas bid, executing before you and taking the spread. Mitigation: use private transaction pools (Flashbots Protect) or focus on chains where MEV is less prevalent.
  • Slippage on large orders â€” a $10,000 ETH swap will move the price more than a $500 swap. Always simulate the trade before executing and reject if expected slippage exceeds your model.
  • Gas price spikes â€” Ethereum gas can 10x in seconds during NFT drops or market volatility. Your profitability check must use real-time gas estimates, not static values.
  • Latency â€” arbitrage opportunities disappear fast. Run your agent in a datacenter geographically close to the RPC nodes you are calling. Add 50ms of latency and many opportunities disappear entirely.
  • Execution risk â€” one leg of the arb might fail or fill at a worse price than expected. Always handle partial fills and have a fallback to unwind the position if the second leg cannot be executed.
