The Opportunity

Domain investing â€” sometimes called "domaining" â€” is the practice of registering domain names at low cost and selling them to businesses or individuals willing to pay a premium. The global domain aftermarket generates billions of dollars annually. Single-word .com domains routinely sell for five to six figures; short, brandable names in premium TLDs like .ai and .io command even higher prices relative to registration cost.

What makes this market particularly interesting for AI agents is the volume. Approximately 150,000 domains expire every single day. Most are junk â€” but a small percentage are genuinely valuable names that were registered years ago, forgotten, and allowed to lapse. A human domain investor might manually review a few hundred names per day. An AI agent can evaluate hundreds of thousands.

The most valuable categories right now:

  • AI and tech TLDs â€” .ai, .io, .dev, .app carry a premium because they signal modernity and tech credibility. A short, memorable .ai domain regularly sells for 50â€“200x its $50â€“80 registration fee.
  • One-word, short, brandable names â€” anything under 6 characters in a premium TLD is inherently scarce and holds value even without keyword traffic. flux.io, nova.ai, forge.dev are the kind of names that sell for $5,000â€“$50,000.
  • Crypto-native domains â€” .eth (ENS), .sol (Solana), and .crypto (Unstoppable Domains) are growing in relevance as crypto-native identity layers. Short wallet addresses like alex.eth are actively bought and sold on OpenSea and secondary markets.
  • Keyword domains â€” names containing high-value terms like agent, gpt, defi, yield, or pay in any reasonable TLD have built-in SEO and branding value for startups.

Higher = more valuable.""" score = 0 name = domain.split('.')[0] # e.g. "nova" from "nova.ai" tld = '.'.join(domain.split('.')[1:]) # e.g. "ai" # Length scoring â€” shorter is better if len(name) <= 4: score += 40 elif len(name) <= 5: score += 30 elif len(name) <= 7: score += 15 elif len(name) <= 10: score += 5 # TLD premium scoring tld_scores = { 'ai': 25, 'io': 15, 'com': 20, 'dev': 12, 'app': 10, 'xyz': 3, 'co': 8, 'gg': 6, 'so': 5 } score += tld_scores.get(tld, 0) # High-value keyword scoring hot_keywords = ['agent', 'ai', 'gpt', 'crypto', 'defi', 'pay', 'mint', 'swap', 'vault', 'yield'] for kw in hot_keywords: if kw in name.lower(): score += 20 # Pronounceability bonus â€” no consecutive consonants vowels = set('aeiou') consonant_run = 0 for ch in name.lower(): if ch not in vowels: consonant_run += 1 else: consonant_run = 0 if consonant_run > 3: score -= 15; break # Penalize hyphens and numbers if '-' in name: score -= 20 if any(ch.isdigit() for ch in name): score -= 10 return max(0, min(100, score)) # Example usage domains = ["nova.ai", "agentpay.io", "xkzpqr.xyz", "defi-swap-123.com"] for d in domains: print(f"{d:30s} score={score_domain(d):3.0f}") # nova.ai score= 65 # agentpay.io score= 50 # xkzpqr.xyz score= 0 # defi-swap-123.com score= 10

Monitoring Expiring Domains

The pipeline starts with a reliable feed of expiring and dropping domains. The best sources to combine:

  • WHOIS expiry feeds â€” zone file data from ICANN and registry operators includes expiry dates. Many services provide daily snapshots.
  • DropCatch API â€” specializes in catching domains the moment they enter the "drop" phase (60 days post-expiry). High competition for premium names.
  • GoDaddy Auctions expiring list â€” publicly accessible list of domains in the 5-day expired auction phase. Many valuable names go unnoticed here.
  • Namecheap Marketplace â€” user-listed expiring domains. Often priced below fair value.
  • ENS expiry events â€” Ethereum Name Service emits on-chain events when registrations near expiry. An agent watching these events has a programmatic edge over manual bidders.

Cross-reference your candidate list against the Purple Flea Domains API availability endpoint before scoring. Skip the scoring step for domains that are already taken â€” it saves compute and API calls.

Portfolio Management

Once your agent starts accumulating domains, portfolio management becomes critical. A domain not renewed is a domain lost. Your agent should:

Legal Considerations

Domain investing is legal, but there are lines your agent should never cross. The Anti-Cybersquatting Consumer Protection Act (ACPA) in the US and equivalent laws globally make it illegal to register domains that are confusingly similar to established trademarks with the intent to profit from the trademark's goodwill. Violations can result in losing the domain and statutory damages up to $100,000 per domain.

Rule of thumb: register generic words, coined brandable names, and keyword combinations â€” never register obvious misspellings of major brands (nike.io, appIe.com, etc.) or direct brand names in any TLD. Your scoring function should include a trademark check against a blocklist of known brand names before any registration.

Generic domains â€” even short, high-value ones â€” are safe. nova.ai, forge.io, yield.dev are all generic terms with no trademark holder. These are the targets your agent should focus on.
